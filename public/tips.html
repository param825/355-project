  • Test soil pH and adjust with lime or sulfur as necessary for plant needs.
  • Add organic matter annually to improve soil structure and nutrient content.
  • Rotate crops to prevent soil depletion and reduce pest infestations.
  • Use companion planting to naturally repel pests (e.g., marigolds deter nematodes and other insects).
  • Encourage beneficial insects like bees and ladybugs by planting diverse flowers and plants.
  • Apply neem oil or diatomaceous earth as non-toxic pest control methods.
  • Water deeply but infrequently to encourage deep root growth and drought tolerance.
  • Avoid overhead watering to prevent leaf diseases, use soaker hoses or drip irrigation instead.
  • Best watering times are early morning to minimize evaporation and fungal growth.
  • Prune during dormancy for most plants to prevent stress and promote healthy growth in the spring.
  • Always use clean, sharp tools to make precise cuts and prevent disease transmission.
  • Remove dead, damaged, or diseased branches to help the plant focus its energy on healthy growth.
  • Understand your plant hardiness zone to choose plants that will thrive in your local climate.
  • Plant bulbs in the fall for spring blooms and plant perennials in early spring or fall when temperatures are cooler.
  • Take advantage of the growing season by starting seeds indoors in late winter for a head start on spring planting.
